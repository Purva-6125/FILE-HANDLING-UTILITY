Java's development began in 1990 under the leadership of James Gosling at Sun Microsystems and was officially released in 1995. Originally named "Oak," it was later renamed "Java," inspired by coffee, symbolizing the developers' energy and productivity. Today, Java is maintained by Oracle Corporation and remains one of the most widely used programming languages globally. Its enduring popularity is a testament to its adaptability, rich feature set, and the active community supporting its development.
